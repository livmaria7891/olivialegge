In a past life, I was a Special Education teacher. Inspired by projects like Sugata Mitra’s “Hole in the Wall” experiment, the endless opportunities to make people’s lives easier with technology drove me pursue a full time career in software development. I have spent the past year doing just that.

As a developer, I have an affinity for clean readable code, finding logical solutions to difficult problems, and creating an enjoyable user experience. When I’m not glued to my keyboard, you might find me café crawling around Capitol Hill, exploring the nearby hiking trails, or making a fool of myself at a local improv class.

My Journey

..and some things I've learned along the way.

A teacher

The years I spent teaching math and reading to students with special needs were just as amazing as they were challenging. My students' struggles with technology taught me the importance of accessibility and creating an intuitive user experience. Teaching allowed me to use instructional data and educational technology to facilitate meaningful learning and help my students achieve their goals.

A Student

Ada Developers Academy is a highly selective, full stack development program for women and non-binary people emphasizing rapid learning, collaboration, and leadership. At Ada, I learned CS Fundamentals, Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, jQuery, and Backbone.js while using Object Oriented Programming techniques and Test Driven Development to develop projects both individually and in small groups.

A Developer

In February, I began an internship at Concur where I have been fully integrated into an agile development team that integrates expense tracking with accounting software like QuickBooks and NetSuite. As a part of this team, I have had the opportunity to write user stories and collaborate with other engineers and the product manager to plan and execute sprints while writing Java code to implement several critical new accounting features.
